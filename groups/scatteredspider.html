01

Executive Summary & Group Overview

Scattered Spider is a financially motivated, English-speaking eCrime collective that emerged in approximately May 2022 out of "The Com," a loose online criminal underground active on Telegram and Discord. Unlike most major ransomware brands, it maintains no proprietary encryptor and no dedicated leak site of its own. It operates as a high-value initial-access and intrusion operator, gaining entry through identity-layer social engineering (help-desk vishing, SIM swapping, MFA fatigue, adversary-in-the-middle phishing) and then deploying ransomware supplied by successive Ransomware-as-a-Service (RaaS) partners: ALPHV/BlackCat (2023), RansomHub and Qilin (2024), and DragonForce (2025 to present).

The group gained international notoriety in September 2023 with back-to-back intrusions at Caesars Entertainment (~$15M ransom paid) and MGM Resorts ($100M+ in losses). Through 2024 and 2025 it rotated systematically through sectors: UK retail (Marks & Spencer, Co-op, Harrods), US insurance (Aflac, Allianz Life), and aviation (Qantas, Hawaiian Airlines, WestJet). As of July 2026 the collective remains operationally active despite a sustained wave of US and UK arrests. Its distinguishing feature within the ecosystem is jurisdictional: members are predominantly young, Western, native English speakers, with no confirmed state nexus, which makes them uniquely exposed to Western law enforcement compared with Russia-based operators.

Analytical bottom line: Scattered Spider is best understood not as a ransomware "group" in the RaaS-operator sense but as a durable, decentralized affiliate and access layer. Its leverage lies in social engineering that defeats identity controls; its structural weakness is OPSEC. The absence of a Russian safe harbor means arrests actually reach members, but the loose-collective model has so far absorbed each enforcement action and resumed activity in a new sector within one to six months.

Lineage & Organizational Heritage

No Predecessor Ransomware Brand

Scattered Spider is not a rebrand of an earlier closed ransomware operation. It evolved from participants in "The Com" (also "The Community"), an English-language criminal underground active via Telegram, Discord, and IRC-style channels that facilitated SIM swapping, fraud, and cryptocurrency theft. Its first large-scale public appearance was the 0ktapus campaign of August 2022, in which the actors (tracked by Group-IB as 0ktapus, and by Okta as Scatter Swine) breached 130+ organizations including Twilio, Cloudflare, and Okta via SMS phishing against Okta SSO credentials.

Vendor Designation Disambiguation

CrowdStrike tracks the group as SCATTERED SPIDER (early phase: Roasted 0ktapus). Analytical consensus nonetheless treats these as substantially overlapping for post-2023 ALPHV-affiliate activity. Treat cross-vendor alias equivalence as CREDIBLE, not CONFIRMED.

Operational Model

Structure

Scattered Spider is a loose, decentralized collective of English-speaking individuals primarily based in the US, UK, Canada, and Europe, not a closed hierarchical operator. Membership is transient and vaguely defined; assessments range from dozens to as many as 1,000 loosely affiliated individuals. It functions as a high-value initial-access and intrusion operator that deploys ransomware from partner platforms rather than maintaining its own infrastructure.

The hallmark is identity-layer exploitation through social engineering, which renders traditional patching insufficient as a standalone control. Where CVE exploitation occurs it is secondary to credential-based access.

Persistence & Lateral Movement
  • Registration of rogue federated identity providers in victim Microsoft Entra (Azure AD) environments, enabling golden SAML attacks; enrollment of attacker devices into MFA policies.
  • RMM tools for persistence: AnyDesk, TeamViewer, ScreenConnect, Teleport, Tailscale VPN; creation of unmanaged VMs to run tools outside EDR coverage.
  • Credential theft: NTDS.dit, Mimikatz, secretsdump.py, DCSync; extraction from CyberArk (psPAS) and HashiCorp Vault; AD enumeration via ADRecon, ADExplorer, SharpHound.
  • Okta permissions abuse (self-assignment to every application); pivoting from on-premises AD to Azure, Salesforce, Workday, Snowflake. Microsoft documented exploitation of the Entra Connect server to pivot from on-prem to cloud in hybrid environments.
  • RattyRAT (Java-based RAT) for long-term stealthy access; Spectre RAT updated variants (Klaviyo, HubSpot, Pure Storage campaigns); commodity WarZone RAT, Vidar, Raccoon. Exfiltration staging to MEGA.nz and AWS S3. (FBI/CISA July 2025 advisory.)
Encryption & Hypervisor Targeting

Scattered Spider maintains no proprietary ransomware; encryption is sourced from partners. ALPHV/BlackCat (2023): Rust, cross-platform, ChaCha20. RansomHub (2024): multi-platform, assessed code sharing from Knight/Cyclops. DragonForce (2025–present): targets VMware ESXi hypervisors specifically; Mandiant assessed "extreme velocity" (initial access to exfiltration to deployment in hours). ESXi targeting is a defining operational signature as of 2025 (FBI/CISA July 2025 advisory); the M&S attack encrypted numerous ESXi servers with ~150GB confirmed stolen.

CIS exclusion, a platform artifact (not group policy): CIS-exclusion behavior belongs to the RaaS platforms Scattered Spider uses (RansomHub avoids CIS, Cuba, North Korea, China; ALPHV excluded CIS), not to Scattered Spider's own tooling, which contains no documented CIS keyboard-layout kill switch. The group has no confirmed CIS targets, but this is a byproduct of partner policy and English-language focus, not an independently enforced exclusion, and it is not a state-coordination indicator.
Decryptor Availability
Confirmed: No public or universal decryptor

No More Ransom lists no decryptor for DragonForce or RansomHub variants used in Scattered Spider attacks. For DragonForce, only victim-specific decryptors obtained via threat-hunting (e.g., S2W/TALON) exist, key-bound and not publicly distributed. RansomHub: no decryptor available per CISA #StopRansomware advisory AA24-242A.

Attribution & State Nexus

Assessment
Confirmed: No state nexus; Western, financially motivated collective

Scattered Spider is assessed by all major threat-intelligence vendors as a financially motivated, Western English-speaking criminal collective with no confirmed operational relationship with Russian state intelligence (FSB, SVR, GRU) or any other nation-state. This is a structural contrast with the Russia-nexus RaaS operators profiled elsewhere in this library.

Evidentiary Basis for the No-Nexus Assessment
  • Demographics: membership predominantly young (~13–25), native English speakers from US, UK, Canada, Europe, inconsistent with documented Russian state-proxied profiles.
  • Victim selection: purely financial; no targeting of CIS adversaries or intelligence-value targets consistent with state tasking.
  • Arrests: US DOJ and UK NCA have charged members as independent cybercriminals with no allegation of state direction; multiple members arrested without apparent interference or safe harbor.
  • CIS exclusion: derives from RaaS platform policy, not independent enforcement, and is a common criminal precaution, not a coordination indicator.
ANALYST INFERENCE / caveat: A May 2026 podcast characterized the ALPHV–Scattered Spider relationship as a potential Russian-intelligence proxy bridge ("FSB watching the dashboard"). This is an unsupported speculative characterization not corroborated by any government advisory, court document, or major vendor, and should not be incorporated into analytical judgments without independent corroboration.

ShinyHunters and the "Scattered LAPSUS$ Hunters" Brand (2025)

The most consequential 2025 development was Scattered Spider's convergence with ShinyHunters, a data-theft and extortion collective active since ~2020 that monetizes stolen data through leak-and-extort operations and the BreachForums marketplace (which ShinyHunters personas have administered). Google Threat Intelligence Group (GTIG) tracks the associated activity not as one group but across multiple clusters: UNC6040 (vishing / initial access), UNC6240 (extortion, which "consistently claimed to be ShinyHunters"), and UNC6395 (the Salesloft Drift OAuth compromise), with further expansion (UNC6661/UNC6671) reported in January 2026. Palo Alto Unit 42 tracks ShinyHunters as Bling Libra.

On August 8, 2025 a Telegram brand, "Scattered LAPSUS$ Hunters," debuted, invoking Scattered Spider, Lapsus$, and ShinyHunters together and teasing a self-built RaaS ("ShinySp1d3r"). Trustwave/LevelBlue SpiderLabs assessed the brand as a federated identity operated by fewer than five core operators behind roughly 30 personas, rather than a corporate merger.

The Salesforce / Salesloft-Drift Extortion Wave
  • Vector 1 (UNC6040 vishing): operators impersonate IT support by phone and trick employees into authorizing a malicious modified Salesforce "Data Loader" connected app (OAuth), then exfiltrate CRM data; later shifted to custom Python tooling.
  • Vector 2 (UNC6395 Salesloft Drift, Aug 8–18, 2025): compromise of Salesloft Drift OAuth tokens reached 700+ organizations, harvesting downstream AWS keys and Snowflake tokens.
  • Division of labor (CREDIBLE): Scattered-Spider-style social engineering supplies access; ShinyHunters handles exfiltration, extortion, and the leak site.
  • Named victims (multi-source): Google, Allianz Life (~1.4M records), Qantas, Cisco, Adidas, Workday, Chanel, LVMH brands (Louis Vuitton, Dior, Tiffany), Cartier, Pandora, Air France-KLM. Drift-specific: Cloudflare, Palo Alto Networks, Zscaler, Proofpoint, Tanium.
  • October 2025 leak site: listed ~39–40 victims and claimed ~1 billion records (attacker claims), set an October deadline, and separately attempted to extort Salesforce itself. Salesforce refused to pay. Data was published hours after the FBI and France's BL2C seized the clearnet BreachForums portal.

Related law enforcement: France arrested four individuals tied to ShinyHunters/BreachForums on June 25, 2025 (personas including "ShinyHunters"); in February 2025 the DOJ indicted Kai West, a British national alleged to be "IntelBroker" and a former BreachForums administrator.

Attribution caution (two-tier): The operational collaboration between Scattered-Spider-aligned access actors and ShinyHunters extortion actors on the Salesforce campaign is CREDIBLE and multi-source. Whether they are the same individuals is ANALYST INFERENCE, not established. GTIG's own language holds that shared infrastructure and TTPs are "plausible [as] associated actors operating within the same communities, rather than indicating a direct operational relationship," and notes UNC6240 actors merely claim the ShinyHunters name to increase extortion pressure. Mandiant deliberately maintains separate UNC clusters rather than equating the groups. Treat "Scattered Spider = ShinyHunters" as an unproven equivalence.
