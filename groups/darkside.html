01

Executive Summary & Group Overview

DarkSide was a Russia-nexus Ransomware-as-a-Service (RaaS) operation active from August 2020 to May 2021. In roughly nine months it extracted over $90 million in Bitcoin from at least 47 paying victims (Elliptic) before its May 2021 attack on Colonial Pipeline triggered a U.S. national emergency declaration and the group's abrupt shutdown. DarkSide cultivated a self-consciously professional and quasi-corporate brand, publishing a code of conduct, prohibiting attacks on healthcare, education, non-profits, and government, and geofencing organizations in Commonwealth of Independent States (CIS) nations.

DarkSide is assessed with high confidence to have continued through the successor operation BlackMatter (July 2021 to November 2021), and subsequently through ALPHV / BlackCat (November 2021 to March 2024). CrowdStrike attributes the operator group to CARBON SPIDER, and Secureworks to GOLD WATERFALL. As of July 2026 the DarkSide brand is inactive, no named DarkSide operator has been publicly indicted or arrested, and no OFAC designation has ever targeted DarkSide operators or wallets.

02

Lineage & Organizational Heritage

Origin

DarkSide has no publicly established ransomware predecessor in the sequential sense of Ryuk to Conti. CrowdStrike assesses the group was built by the CARBON SPIDER adversary, active since 2013 in other financially motivated operations before pivoting to Big Game Hunting ransomware in summer 2020. The RaaS platform was formally advertised by the actor "darksupp" from November 2020. DarkSide 2.0 followed in March 2021, adding faster multithreaded encryption, a Linux and VMware ESXi variant, and a VoIP calling feature for affiliate pressure.

Vendor Tracking Designations: Disambiguation
Designation caution. CARBON SPIDER is CrowdStrike's designation for the FIN7 adversary (aliases include GOLD NIAGARA, ELBRUS, and Sangria Tempest). By attributing DarkSide and BlackMatter to CARBON SPIDER, CrowdStrike ties the operators to the broad FIN7 organization. This is a broader attribution than Secureworks' GOLD WATERFALL, which Secureworks tracks as its own DarkSide/BlackMatter cluster rather than folding it into GOLD NIAGARA (its FIN7 designation). Vendors therefore do not fully agree on whether the DarkSide operators are FIN7 or a distinct but related cluster. Do not conflate CARBON SPIDER (a parent-organization designation) with a DarkSide-specific attribution.

DarkSide to BlackMatter (Anchor Relationship)
Confirmed  •  High confidence, multi-source

The DarkSide to BlackMatter link is corroborated across cryptographic code analysis, TTP overlap, operator self-statement, and targeting evolution. The only meaningful dispute is framing (strict rebrand versus close successor), not the existence of continuity.

Pillar 1  •  Confirmed
Cryptographic Match
Fabian Wosar (Emsisoft) analyzed a leaked BlackMatter decryptor and found the RSA and Salsa20 implementations, including a custom Salsa20 matrix previously unique to DarkSide, were an exact copy. Emsisoft concluded the two are effectively the same. Strongest single pillar.
Pillar 2  •  Credible
TTP Continuity (with caveat)
Sophos found wallpaper reset, multithreaded encryption, and UAC bypass "technically very similar" to DarkSide, but stated explicitly this is "not a simple case of rebranding." BlackMatter code is not identical and incorporates features from REvil and LockBit.
Pillar 3  •  Credible (self-report)
Operator Statement
A BlackMatter representative told Recorded Future's Dmitry Smilyanets: "We are familiar with the DarkSide team from working together in the past but we are not them, although we are intimate with their ideas." Self-serving and contradicted by the code analysis.
Pillar 4  •  Circumstantial / High
Targeting Avoidance
BlackMatter explicitly excluded oil and gas (pipelines, refineries), the exact industry whose targeting caused DarkSide's downfall. Deliberate avoidance consistent with continuity of personnel who learned from the Colonial failure.

Vendor positions. Corroborating: Emsisoft (strongest), CrowdStrike (CARBON SPIDER covers both), Secureworks (GOLD WATERFALL covers both), Recorded Future (successor), Flashpoint (possible rebrand), Mandiant (actor-level alignment, Kimberly Goody, May 2021). Caveating: Sophos (connection real, rejects "simple rebranding"). No vendor denies the connection.

BlackMatter to ALPHV / BlackCat (Extension Relationship)
Credible  •  Moderate confidence; nature of link disputed

The FBI's ALPHV flash advisory (CU-000167-MW, April 2022) stated that many ALPHV developers and money launderers are linked to DarkSide and BlackMatter. Cisco Talos documented shared domain, IP, and tooling overlap between a September 2021 BlackMatter attack and a December 2021 BlackCat attack, assessing at moderate confidence that BlackMatter affiliates were early BlackCat adopters.

Disputed nature of the link. Whether ALPHV represents former DarkSide/BlackMatter core developers (Emsisoft's Brett Callow leans this way) or migrating affiliates (Cisco Talos infrastructure evidence, and ALPHV's own claim to have been "advertisers" of prior RaaS) is genuinely unresolved. The Canadian Centre for Cyber Security records the dispute without resolving it. The link is well corroborated; the core-versus-affiliate distinction is not.
Cluster-assessment flag. No single vendor has published one formal assessment naming the complete DarkSide to BlackMatter to ALPHV chain as a unified, code-continuous lineage. The three-generation chain is an analyst-community consensus assembled from stage-specific statements: hop one (DarkSide to BlackMatter) is HIGH confidence and code-grounded; hop two (BlackMatter to ALPHV) is MODERATE and rests on infrastructure overlap plus testimony. Neither Mandiant nor Recorded Future has published a formal full-chain cluster attribution.
03

Operational Model

RaaS Structure & Affiliate Management

DarkSide ran a closed-operator RaaS. Core developers maintained the ransomware, the administration panel, the leak-site infrastructure, and the negotiation portal, while vetted affiliates paid for access and conducted intrusions independently. Recruitment ran through the actor "darksupp" on exploit.in and xss.is from November 2020. Affiliates passed an interview before receiving a build panel that allowed per-victim customization (encryption mode, target scope, ransom-note content).

Mandiant identified at least five Russian-speaking actors who were or had been DarkSide affiliates, some of whom concurrently partnered with BABUK and REvil (SODINOKIBI). Affiliates were prohibited from targeting hospitals, schools, universities, non-profits, government entities, or any organization in CIS nations.

Elliptic's blockchain analysis found the core operator wallet received approximately 17% ($15.5 million) of the tracked $90 million, with 83% ($74.7 million) flowing to affiliate wallets, broadly consistent with the sliding 10% to 25% split.

Negotiation & Extortion Mechanics
  • Demands were tailored per victim from research into company financials and decision-makers. Observed demand range: $200,000 to $20 million (Check Point). Average payment: $1.9 million (Elliptic, 47 victims).
  • Payment deadlines used countdown timers; refusal to engage within two to three days triggered escalation.
  • Escalation progression (Unit 42): threatening emails to employees, then calls to senior executives, then threats to contact customers and press, then DDoS against external websites.
  • Double extortion was standard (exfiltration before encryption, publication threat on DarkSide Leaks). Triple-extortion DDoS pressure emerged in 2021.
  • Novel tactic (April 2021): operators announced willingness to share pre-leak intelligence with traders shorting NASDAQ-listed victim companies.
  • In at least one documented case an affiliate obtained the victim's cyber-insurance policy and refused to reduce the demand below the known policy limit.
Sanctions-exposure signal (November 2020). DarkSide announced intent to host infrastructure in Iran, prompting ransomware negotiation firms to place the group on restricted lists over potential OFAC exposure. This is atypical for a Kremlin-tolerated group and complicates a purely Russia-nexus reading (see Section 08).

Persistence, Lateral Movement & Tooling
  • Persistence: ransomware registered as a Windows service under a pseudo-random 8-character lowercase hex name derived from MAC address or MachineGuid; legitimate RMM tools (AnyDesk, TeamViewer) for remote access; SMOKEDHAM .NET backdoor (UNC2465); Cobalt Strike BEACON via HTTPS and SMB (UNC2628); NGROK to expose RDP/WinRM.
  • Lateral movement: RDP with valid domain credentials; PsExec deployment to host lists; Cobalt Strike SMB BEACON; ADRecon, BloodHound/SharpHound, Advanced IP Scanner for reconnaissance; Mimikatz for LSASS credential dumping; F-Secure C3 framework proxying C2 through the Slack API (UNC2628).
  • Exfiltration: Rclone to cloud services (pCloud, MegaSync).

Decryptor status (CREDIBLE). Bitdefender released a free DarkSide decryptor on January 11, 2021, exploiting a key-reuse flaw. Publicizing it tipped off DarkSide, who patched key generation the next day, so it recovers files only for victims encrypted before roughly January 2021 and is ineffective against the mature (Colonial-era) variant. No universal free DarkSide decryptor exists as of July 2026. Note: the October 2021 free BlackMatter decryptor was from Emsisoft (Fabian Wosar), not Bitdefender.
CIS Exclusion / Language Check

The DarkSide binary contains a configurable system-language check (T1614.001). When enabled, it calls GetSystemDefaultUILanguage and GetUserDefaultLangID and halts on detection of Russian, Ukrainian, Belarusian, Georgian, Tatar, Kazakh, Tajik, Kyrgyz, Romanian/Moldova, Armenian, Turkmen, Azerbaijani, and Arabic (Syria) language identifiers, writing "This is a Russian-Speaking System, Exit" to its log. Mandiant notes the check is enabled or disabled per affiliate build and is therefore not present in all samples.

Anti-Recovery & Linux / ESXi Variant
  • Anti-recovery: shadow-copy deletion via obfuscated PowerShell and WMI; Recycle Bin emptying; service stop and deletion targeting vss, sql, backup, veeam, sophos, memtas, mepocs and related services; process termination of databases, Office applications, and browsers.
  • UAC bypass: pre-Windows 10 via slui.exe handler hijack; Windows 10 and later via the CMSTPLUA COM interface.
  • Linux / ESXi (DarkSide 2.0, March 2021): targets .vmdk, .vmsd, .vmsn on VMware ESXi; uses esxcli vm process kill to halt VMs before encryption; also targets Synology and OMV NAS. Some affiliates failed to stop ESXi daemons cleanly, producing interlaced encrypted and unencrypted data, a documented operational error.
  • Code signing: early samples signed with a Cobalt Strike stager certificate to bypass execution restrictions.

DarkSide administrators forwarded 63.7 BTC (about 85%) to the affiliate wallet, consistent with a roughly 15% operator cut; the majority moved out of the initial wallet by May 9.

FBI investigators traced the funds through at least 23 cryptocurrency accounts before they settled in a wallet for which the FBI held the private key.

Seizure
On May 27 to 28, 2021 the FBI seized 63.7 BTC via a warrant from Magistrate Judge Laurel Beeler (N.D. Cal.); DOJ announced the seizure June 7, 2021. The dollar value had fallen to about $2.3 million from $4.4 million due to a Bitcoin price decline.
Key forensic nuance (Elliptic, Tom Robinson). The seized funds came from the affiliate wallet, not the DarkSide operator wallet. The $2.3 million figure is a price-crash artifact: 63.7 BTC was roughly 84% to 85% of the coins Colonial paid but only about half the original dollar value at seizure. Approximately $2.1 million of the original payment was never recovered.
Laundering & Attribution Gaps
  • Elliptic conducted on-chain tracking to identify DarkSide wallets; Chainalysis assisted the FBI in tracing the Colonial payment; TRM Labs also published specific DarkSide on-chain analysis (tracking the 75 BTC withdrawal on May 8 and 113.5 BTC leaving the DarkSide wallet on May 13).
  • No vendor has publicly named the specific off-ramp exchange or mixer used to launder DarkSide proceeds. Chainalysis stated funds moved to a mixer whose name it could not reveal due to an ongoing investigation. Public detail does not extend beyond the Bitcoin addresses in DOJ court documents.
CONFIRMED  •  No OFAC action on DarkSide. No OFAC designation has ever targeted DarkSide operators or specific DarkSide wallets. The September 2021 SUEX and November 2021 Chatex sanctions are frequently but incorrectly linked to DarkSide: the documented ransomware nexus for SUEX is Ryuk, Conti, and Maze, and for the Chatex action, REvil. Neither designation names DarkSide. Avoid repeating the SUEX/DarkSide misconception.

Victim Profile & Targeting

Scale & Distribution
  • 99 organizations infected per DarkTracer (cited by Elliptic, May 2021); 47 paid (about 47%). Active over roughly nine months across more than 15 countries.
  • Geography: majority U.S.-based (Mandiant, CSIS), with European victims including Brenntag (Germany) and Toshiba Tec (France office). No known CIS-nation victims, consistent with the programmatic prohibition.
  • Model: Big Game Hunting. DarkSide deliberately targeted larger for-profit enterprises able to pay multi-million-dollar ransoms, calibrating demands to victim revenue and researching decision-makers and insurance limits.
Sectors

Mandiant documented financial services, legal, manufacturing, professional services, retail, and technology. The FBI/CISA advisory (AA21-131A) additionally noted manufacturing, legal, insurance, healthcare, and energy. Stated exclusions: CIS nations, hospitals, schools, universities, non-profits, and government entities.

07

Law Enforcement & Regulatory Response

DOJ Seizure (June 2021)

On June 7, 2021 DOJ announced the seizure of 63.7 BTC (about $2.3 million), the majority of Colonial's ransom, from a DarkSide-associated wallet. The action was executed by the Ransomware and Digital Extortion Task Force under criminal and civil forfeiture statutes (computer intrusion, money laundering), not sanctions authority, and announced by Deputy Attorney General Lisa Monaco. The FBI held the private key; the method of key acquisition was not disclosed.

Indictments, Sanctions & Rewards
As of July 2026: no named DarkSide operator has ever been publicly indicted or arrested, and no OFAC designation has targeted DarkSide operators or wallets. The identities of "darksupp" and the CARBON SPIDER / GOLD WATERFALL core operators remain unknown in the public domain.
  • State Department rewards (November 2021): up to $10 million for information identifying or locating DarkSide leadership, plus up to $5 million for information leading to the arrest or conviction of any participant. Still active.
  • ALPHV-lineage arrests (not DarkSide core): the only related arrests to date, Ryan Goldberg and Kevin Martin, who pleaded guilty (2024 to 2025) to deploying ALPHV/BlackCat, are U.S.-based affiliates and negotiators, not the Russia-based DarkSide developers.
Advisory & Infrastructure
  • FBI/CISA Joint Advisory AA21-131A (May 11, 2021): issued in the wake of Colonial Pipeline, confirming the RaaS model, providing IOCs, and urging segmentation, tested isolated backups, and MFA.
  • May 2021 infrastructure loss: DarkSide claimed its servers and payment-panel funds were lost "at the request of law enforcement." No government agency has ever officially confirmed executing this takedown, and some analysts assess it may have been an exit-scam cover story timed to U.S. pressure. It remains unattributed.
  • International action: no formal Europol or Interpol-led joint operation against DarkSide was announced. The public record is dominated by unilateral U.S. actions.

Attribution & State Nexus

Summary Assessment
Credible  •  Tacit state tolerance, not confirmed direction

No confirmed state direction or formal intelligence-sharing relationship has been established between DarkSide and the Russian government. The group is assessed as a criminal enterprise operating under what CSIS and others characterize as tacit Kremlin tolerance: permitted within Russia's permissive environment for cybercrime targeting non-CIS entities. President Biden publicly stated there was no evidence of Russian government involvement in the Colonial attack itself.

Trajectory Assessment

Status: Inactive since May 2021. DarkSide's shutdown was rapid and externally precipitated by the geopolitical fallout of Colonial Pipeline. The group cut off a clearly ascending revenue trajectory (roughly $17.5 million in its final three months), pledged decryptors to active victims, and reported its payment-server funds transferred to an unknown account. Multiple researchers noted the drained funds could equally represent an exit scam. Core operators are assessed to have continued through BlackMatter and then ALPHV / BlackCat.

Documented Intelligence Gaps
  • Operator identities: "darksupp" and the CARBON SPIDER / GOLD WATERFALL core operators remain unidentified and unindicted.
  • Off-ramp infrastructure: the specific exchanges, mixers, or OTC desks used to cash out DarkSide proceeds are not publicly documented.
  • State-nexus specificity: the precise nature of any Kremlin relationship (tacit tolerance versus occasional tasking) is unconfirmed; no public intelligence-community assessment addresses DarkSide's state relationship directly.
